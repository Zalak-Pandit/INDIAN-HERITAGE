About Haryana

Haryana is an Indian state located in the northern part of the country. It was carved out after the linguistic reorganisation of Punjab on 1 November 1966. The state is rich in history, monuments, heritage, flora and fauna and tourism, with a well-developed economy, national highways and state roads. It is bordered by Punjab and Himachal Pradesh to the north, by Rajasthan to the west and south, while river Yamuna forms its eastern border with Uttar Pradesh. During the Vedic era, Haryana was the site of the Kuru Kingdom, one of India's great Mahajanapadas.

Discover the most engaging places

Gurgaon

Once little more than a dull and comparatively unknown suburb of the national capital region, today, Gurgaon is not only one of the most urbanised cities of India, but it also boasts of a skyline that can easily rival those of Hong Kong and Singapore. Easily accessible from Delhi and Noida.

Kurukshetra

A city in the state of Haryana, Kurukshetra, also known as Dharmakshetra is known as the "Land of the Bhagavad Gita". Situated 170 km north to Delhi, the place is famous for the battle between the Kauvaras and the Pandavas in the Hindu epic, Mahabharata. It is the place where Lord Krishna is said to have recited the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna. Thaneswar, whose urban area is merged with Kurukshetra, is a pilgrimage site with many locations attributed to Mahabharata.

Faridabad

Faridabad is Haryana's largest city and its major industrial hub as well. Faridabad is the main part of district Faridabad which has two divisions - Faridabad and Ballabgarh. The city shares borders with New Delhi on its north, Gurgaon to its west and Uttar Pradesh on its south west ends.

Taste the flavours of Punjab

Bathua Raita

Bathua Ka Raita is a popular and delicious variant of Raita, which is a yogurt-based accompaniment for any meal in Indian cuisine. It could be part of an elaborate meal like thali, an everyday meal, or served with any rice dish like pulao or biryani. It is a creamy, refreshing, winter favorite that is so easy and quick to make.

Besan Roti

Besan roti, a delicious gluten-free flatbread made with chickpea flour, is loaded with protein and fiber. It needs needs just two essential ingredients and is so easy to make. Serve it as a healthy snack or for dinner. It's a wonderful, gluten-free substitute for a roti made with wheat flour and it is low-carb-friendly as well!

Zarda Pulao

Whether you call it Zarda Pulao or Meethe Chawal, one thing remains the same: this bright and sunny dish is perfect for festive occasions. This is a fragrant, sweet pulao or sweet rice made with basmati rice, nuts, dry fruits, and saffron. Best of all, you only need 8 ingredients and 30 minutes of active time to make my Zarda recipe at home.

Singri Sabzi

Sangri Ki Sabji is a traditional dish made during festivals, weddings and even otherwise. Simple and quick to make this dish is a delicacy and people of Rajasthan love it. Sangri has otherwise a slight bitter taste and to balance this raisins are added. To balance the spice and the heat, yogurt is added to this dish which is high in fiber.

Handicrafts from the villages

Knitting

These are iconic and traditional, woven with intricate patterns that represent the various tribes. Each tribe has its unique designs and motifs.

Pottery

Pottery in Haryana ranges from simple pots meant for daily use to highly artistic decorative pieces. In Jhajjar, in Rohtak district, pitchers made with clay are greatly valued as the clay imparts sweet taste to the water stored in the pitcher.

Cane & Bamboo

Bamboo craft is a period aged craft practiced widely due to its immense merits. Haryanas mudas or the low down stools are made from the nearby obtainable munj grass.
