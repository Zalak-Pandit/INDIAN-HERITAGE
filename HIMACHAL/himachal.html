About Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh is a state in the northen part of India. Situated in the Western Himalayas, is characterised by an extreme landscape featuring several peaks and extensive river systems. Himachal Pradesh is also known as Dev Bhoomi or Dev Bhumi, meaning 'Land of Gods' and Veer Bhoomi which means 'Land of the Brave'.

Discover the most engaging places

Shimla

Shimla is the capital and the largest city of Himachal Pradesh. The colonial architecture and churches, the temples, and the natural environment of the city attract tourists. Major city centre's attractions include the Shri Hanuman Jakhu (Statue), Jakhu Temple, Viceregal Lodge, Christ Church, Mall Road, The Ridge and Annadale.

Manali

Manali is a town, near Kullu town in Kullu dstrict in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. Manali is named after Manu, the progenitor of humanity in Hinduism. The name Manali is regarded as the derivative of Manu-Alaya ('the abode of Manu'). Manali is a popular tourist destination in India and serves as the gateway to the Lahaul and Spiti district as well as the city of Leh in Ladakh.

Dharamshala

Dharamashala is a town in Himachal Pradesh. The town is located in the Kangra Valley, in the shadow of the Dhauladhar range of the Himalayas at an altitude of 1,457 metres. Dharamashala is a Hindi word derived from Sanskrit that is a compound of words dharma and shala literally "House or place of Dharma". Himachal Pradesh Cricket Association Stadium is a cricket stadium located in Dharamshala. Situated at an altitude of 1317m, it is one of the highest altitude cricket stadiums.

Taste the flavours of Himachal

Madra

Madra is a curd and legume based curry from the himachali cuisine. Usually the beans that are added in madra recipe are chickpeas, kidney beans and black eyed beans. Madra is one of the dishes that is served in a himachali food feast along with various other dishes. In himachali language, this himachali food festival is called as Dham.

Tudkiya Bhath

The pulao dish, Tudkiya Bhath is native to Chamba. However, it is different from any other pulao. This dish is made with rice, ghee, lentils, potatoes, yoghurt, onions, garlic, ginger and tomatoes. Cardamoms, cinnamon and bay leaves are added to give it flavour.

Bhey

This dish is made with lotus stems. Other ingredients are finely sliced stems, gram flour, ginger, garlic, onion and spices such as cayenne pepper, turmeric powder, chilli powder and coriander powder.

Siddu

Siddu is a steamed bun. It is cooked with a dough consisting of wheat flour, salt, sugar, ghee and yeast and a filling of pates made from ground walnuts, almonds and poppy seeds.

Handicrafts from the villages

Chamba Rumaal

The Chamba Rumal is an embroidered handicraft. It is a common item of gift during marriages with detailed patterns in bright and pleasing colour schemes. The materials used still consist of muslin, malmal, khaddar (a coarse fabric), fine charcoal or brush, and silk threads without knots.

Metal Crafts

The metal craft of Himachal Pradesh stands exemplary as the antique metal statuettes are one of the most significant aspects in many temples of Himachal Pradesh. The statues of gods and goddesses also appear as 'mohras' or in metal plaques.

Jewellery of Himachal Pradesh

Tribal Jewellery of Himachal PradeshThe jewellery of Himachal Pradesh is very unique as well as varied. Chunky bead and metal jewellery of the hill people is very common as well as popular in this region.

Kullu Dussehra

A journal is a place to record new things you have discovered while exploring various places you visit.

Every Himachal Pradesh festival is a vibrant blend of deep-rooted traditions, colourful rituals, and unwavering faith that resonates through every celebration. Among these, 'Kullu Dussehra' stands out as a mesmerising spectacle and one of the state's grandest festivals. Unlike the Dussehra celebrations seen elsewhere in the country, Kullu Dussehra unfolds as a week-long extravaganza. This Himachal Pradesh festival kicked off with a revered procession honouring Lord Raghunath, the local deity.

Kullu Dussehra is the renowned International Mega Dussehra festival observed in the month of October in Himachal Pradesh state in northern India. Wherein more than 4-5 lakh people visit the fair from all across the Globe. It is celebrated in the Dhalpur maidan in the Kullu valley. Dussehra at Kullu commences on the tenth day of the rising moon, i.e. on 'Vijay Dashmi' day itself and continues for seven days.







Dussehra of Kullu has its own essence, and it is one of the prime attractions for many tourists not only in India but for international travellers too. Kullu’s Dussehra is celebrated in the traditional and customary manner which drives in so many people to Kullu during this period. This is the most awaited event of Himachal Pradesh. As the procession sets the festive tone, it paves the way for days filled with jubilant singing, spirited dancing, and heartwarming celebrations. It's a spectacle that captures the essence and beauty of Himachal's rich cultural landscape.
