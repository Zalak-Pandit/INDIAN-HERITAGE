Discover the most engaging places

Kochi(cochin)

Cemetery was built in 1944 to commemorate and pay homage to those brave hearts who were martyred in the World War II. Trek on the Garrison Hills to reach the War Cemetery, a memorial dedicated to British and Japanese soldiers who lost their lives for the Burma attack.

Munnar

offers excitement to wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers in the form of Intanki Wildlife Sanctuary. Spread over a vast 200 sq km area, the wildlife reserve houses exotic beings such sloth bears, wild dogs, flying squirrels, barking deer and black storks among many more!

Alapuza

Sprawling over a large area of 176 ha, the park serves as a conservation area for the flora and fauna of the northeast India. The low-lying area of the park is a suitable topography for aquatic birds, and there are almost 500 bird species in the region which include Patridges, Parrotbill, Laughingthrush, Nuttatch and Fulvetta.

Iddiyapam

Ambotik is actually two words, ambot which means tangy/sour & tik which means hot/spicy in Konkani

Nadan Kozhi Varuthathu

A pickle-like blend of ingredients called balchao, is crucial to many of Portuguese Goa’s meat dishes, and usually incorporates Goa’s other passion, feni, a potent alcoholic brew made from either cashew nuts or palm fruit.

Handicrafts from the villages

Shawls

These are iconic and traditional, woven with intricate patterns that represent the various tribes. Each tribe has its unique designs and motifs.

Beadwork

Bead necklaces and jewelry are integral to Naga traditional attire,showcasing vibrant colors and intricate patterns.

Dimapur

Wood Carving: Naga artisans are skilled in wood carving, creating beautiful sculptures,masks, and other decorative items that often depict tribal myths and folklore.
