About Nagaland

Nagaland, located in the northeastern region of India, is known for its vibrant tribal culture and rich traditions. The state is home to 16 major tribes, each with its unique customs, festivals, and dialects. Surrounded by lush hills and picturesque landscapes, Nagaland is a haven for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts. Visitors can explore its diverse flora and fauna, traditional handicrafts, and the warm hospitality of its people.

Discover the most engaging places

Kohima

The Kohima War Cemetery was built in 1944 to commemorate and pay homage to those brave hearts who were martyred in the World War II. Trek on the Garrison Hills to reach the War Cemetery, a memorial dedicated to British and Japanese soldiers who lost their lives for the Burma attack.

Intanki

Nagaland offers excitement to wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers in the form of Intanki Wildlife Sanctuary. Spread over a vast 200 sq km area, the wildlife reserve houses exotic beings such sloth bears, wild dogs, flying squirrels, barking deer and black storks among many more!

Dimapur

Sprawling over a large area of 176 ha, the park serves as a conservation area for the flora and fauna of the northeast India. The low-lying area of the park is a suitable topography for aquatic birds, and there are almost 500 bird species in the region which include Patridges, Parrotbill, Laughingthrush, Nuttatch and Fulvetta.

Taste the flavours of nagaland

Smoked Pork with Bamboo Shoot

Smoked pork is a staple in Naga cuisine, often cooked with bamboo shoots, which adds a unique, tangy flavor to the dish.

Hinkejvu

Hinkejvu is a simple yet delicious dish made with colocasia (taro), shredded cabbage, mustard leaves, and smoked pork.

Aikibeye

Aikibeye is a dish made with mustard leaf and colocasia roots, often cooked with pork fat to enhance its flavor.

Galho

Galho is a Naga version of khichdi, made with rice, vegetables, and meat. It's a comforting, nutritious meal often enjoyed during colder months

Handicrafts from the villages

Naga Shawls

These are iconic and traditional, woven with intricate patterns that represent the various tribes. Each tribe has its unique designs and motifs.

Beadwork

Bead necklaces and jewelry are integral to Naga traditional attire,showcasing vibrant colors and intricate patterns.

Dimapur

Wood Carving: Naga artisans are skilled in wood carving, creating beautiful sculptures,masks, and other decorative items that often depict tribal myths and folklore.

History and Significance

Inception:
The festival was first organized in 2000 by the Government of Nagaland to encourage inter-tribal interaction and promote the cultural heritage of the state. It is now one of the largest and most important cultural events in Northeast India.


Cultural Heritage:
The festival aims to preserve and promote the rich traditions, customs, and practices of the Naga tribes, who are known for their distinct and vibrant cultures.

Activities and Attraction

Cultural Performances:
Various Naga tribes perform traditional dances, folk songs, and music. Each tribe showcases its unique cultural heritage through colorful attire and energetic performances.


Handicrafts and Cuisine:
Stalls selling traditional Naga handicrafts, handloom products, and local delicacies are a highlight. Visitors can savor authentic Naga cuisine, which is known for its use of unique herbs and spices.

Significance for Nagaland

Economic Impact:
The festival provides a substantial boost to the local economy, particularly for artisans, performers, and small businesses.


Cultural Preservation:
It plays a crucial role in preserving and promoting the traditional cultures of the Naga tribes,ensuring that these customs are passed down to future generations.
