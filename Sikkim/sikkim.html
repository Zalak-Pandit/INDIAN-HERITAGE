About Sikkim

Sikkim, nestled in the eastern Himalayas, is a picturesque state known for its stunning landscapes and cultural diversity. Its capital, Gangtok, offers breathtaking views of the majestic Kangchenjunga, the world's third-highest peak. Sikkim boasts a rich tapestry of cultures, with vibrant festivals and serene Buddhist monasteries. The state's diverse ecosystems range from subtropical forests to alpine meadows, home to unique flora and fauna.

Destinations

Discover the most engaging places

Gangtok

Gangtok, the capital city of Sikkim, is a vibrant and picturesque destination nestled in the eastern Himalayas. Known for its stunning views, rich culture, and serene environment, Gangtok offers a blend of natural beauty and modern amenities. The city's bustling MG Marg offers a pedestrian-friendly area filled with shops and eateries.

Tsongmo Lake

Tsongmo Lake is revered by the local Sikkimese people and holds religious importance. During the winter months, the lake remains frozen, while in summer, it reflects the surrounding snow-capped mountains and alpine forests, creating a picturesque landscape. The lake's waters are sourced from the melting snow of the surrounding mountains.

Buddha Park

Buddha Park (also known as Tathagata Tsal) is a prominent Buddhist site located in Ravangla, in the southern part of Sikkim. The park is home to a magnificent 130-foot statue of Buddha, which was consecrated by the Dalai Lama in 2013 to mark the 2550th birth anniversary of Gautama Buddha.

Taste the flavours of sikkim

Sael Roti

Deep-fried bread made from rice flour, commonly enjoyed during festivals. Crispy on the outside and soft inside.

Tongba

A traditional alcoholic beverage made from fermented millet. Consumed with warm water, it is sipped through a bamboo straw.

Kinema

Fermented soybean dish cooked with spices and often served with rice. Known for its unique, strong flavor.

Thukpa

A hearty noodle soup with vegetables and meat, typically chicken or beef. Influenced by Tibetan cuisine.

Handicrafts from the villages

Thangka Paintings

Traditional Tibetan Buddhist scroll paintings depicting religious deities, mandalas, and scenes.Detailed and colorful, these paintings are often used for meditation and religious rituals.

Ceramics and Pottery

Handmade ceramics and pottery with traditional patterns. Includes items such as decorative pots, vases, and bowls..

Traditional Sikkimese Attire

Includes garments like the Khamtsen (traditional dress for women) and the Bhutia (traditional dress for men). Often adorned with elaborate embroidery and vibrant patterns.

The Saga Dawa Festival

Saga Dawa is one of the most significant festivals in Sikkim, especially for the Buddhist community. It commemorates three important events in the life of Lord Buddha: his birth, enlightenment, and death (Parinirvana).

History and Significance


Buddha's Life Events: Saga Dawa is observed to honor the birth, enlightenment (Bodhi), and death (Parinirvana) of Lord Buddha, all of which are believed to have occurred on the same day of the lunar month.

Spiritual Importance:
The festival is a time for Buddhists to engage in meritorious activities, such as making offerings, performing prayers, and engaging in acts of generosity and compassion.

Celebrations

Monastic Rituals:
Monasteries across Sikkim perform special prayers and rituals. Monks chant sacred texts, and there are elaborate ceremonies dedicated to Buddha.

Circumambulation:

Devotees participate in a ritual called Kora, which involves circumambulating around sacred sites, stupas, and monasteries. One of the most famous Kora routes is around the Tsuklakhang Palace and Monastery in Gangtok.

Cultural Aspects
Prayer Flags:
During Saga Dawa, colorful prayer flags are hung around homes, monasteries, and public places. These flags are believed to carry prayers and blessings to all sentient beings.

Butter Lamps:
Lighting butter lamps is a common practice during the festival. The lamps symbolize the dispelling of darkness and ignorance. Traditional Attire:
People dress in their traditional best, with many wearing vibrant and elaborate costumes.
